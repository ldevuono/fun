heatwave

there’s a blood stain on the sidewalk outside my house. i can see it from my kitchen window. when the sun beats in at 11am, it shines an unnatural crimson onto the linoleum. at first it was sticky, wet wine-coloured, glistening, glossy in its thick near-black. flies hovered around, dipping their toes in. i wanted to join them, to stick my tongue in the pool, but instead i walked past it, turned around and walked past it again. i found a plastic container full of iridescent glass pebbles on my walk yesterday. i put them into a shallow blue and purple dish i found abandoned weeks before. the tumbling, shifting sound they made as i washed them, transferring them from container to sieve to towel to dish, woke the cat. all my walks lately have felt weighed down, like my limbs are too heavy, every step a slog. when i get home i immediately want to leave again. when the sun’s about to set i’ll head out for another walk, down the street, up the alley, to the old gas station on the hill and back. when i get home it’ll be dark. i’ll sidestep around the stain, shimmering in the uncanny heat. i’ll move my hand through the dish of cool glass beads and imagine relief.
