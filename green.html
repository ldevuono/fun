silver

There’s a stuffed deer in the corner of my bedroom

There’s a blood stain on the sidewalk outside

Feet from where a rabbit sat silent

glowing in the blue light of the moon

I watched him, still and silver in the cold

Standing in the porch light, looking at the snow

Early this morning while the house was asleep

I crept up the unfinished stairs

Slid open the glass door and met the screaming dawn

The sky opened like a prayer
