blurred world

I work like a gardener sprinkling seeds over pooled oil,

wandering thoughtless through a blue morning.

Blurred world pale beyond my door

I read the same words over and over


How many grains of sand make a heap?

My mind a bank of tight packed snow

I fall backwards into it, get caught before landing,

Slipping down the sidewalk, squeezing nothing out of stone.


Torn hole in wire mesh, flakes flit against the glass

Dry branches reaching dead out of the snow

Quivering, withered and half buried,

They tear towards the blinding sky
